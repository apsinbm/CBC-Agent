## Q: What's the best time to visit Bermuda?

A: Peak season is May through mid-October with temperatures of 75-85°F. Bermuda enjoys mild winters (60-70°F) and warm summers year-round. Hurricane season runs June 1 to November 30, though direct hits are rare.

## Q: What currency is used in Bermuda?

A: Bermuda uses the Bermuda Dollar (BMD), which is pegged 1:1 with the US Dollar. US Dollars are accepted everywhere. Most establishments accept major credit cards.

## Q: What are the tipping customs?

A: Standard tipping in Bermuda:
- Restaurants: 17-20% (check if service is included)
- Taxis: 15-20%
- Hotel staff: $2-5 per service
- Spa services: 15-20%

## Q: What type of electrical outlets are used?

A: Bermuda uses Type A and Type B outlets (same as the United States) with 120V power. No adapter needed for US visitors; others may need converters.

## Q: What time zone is Bermuda in?

A: Bermuda is in the Atlantic Time Zone (AST), which is one hour ahead of Eastern Time. Bermuda observes Daylight Saving Time.

## Q: Do I need a visa to visit Bermuda?

A: US, UK, and Canadian citizens don't need visas for stays up to 90 days. All visitors need a valid passport. Check current requirements as policies may change.

## Q: What are the emergency numbers?

A: 
- Emergency (Police/Fire/Ambulance): 911
- Police (non-emergency): 295-0011
- King Edward VII Hospital: 236-2345
- Poison Control: 236-2345

## Q: Is tap water safe to drink?

A: Yes! Bermuda's tap water is safe to drink. Most water comes from rain collected on roofs and stored in tanks, then filtered and treated.

## Q: What should I pack?

A: Essentials include:
- Resort casual and beach wear
- Whites/creams for tennis (if playing)
- Light jacket for evenings
- Sunscreen and sunglasses
- Formal jacket for men (Thursday/Saturday dinners)
- Reef-safe sunscreen

## Q: How do I get around the island?

A: Options include:
- Taxis (no Uber/Lyft available)
- Scooter rentals (popular but requires comfort with left-side driving)
- Public buses (exact change required)
- Ferry service (scenic option for some routes)

## Q: What side of the road do they drive on?

A: Bermuda drives on the left side of the road (British system). The speed limit is 35 kph (about 22 mph) island-wide.

## Q: Can I use my cell phone?

A: Most international cell plans work in Bermuda, but check roaming charges with your provider. Free Wi-Fi is available at the club.

## Q: What language is spoken?

A: English is the official language. You'll hear a distinctive Bermudian accent and some local expressions.

## Q: Are there any cultural customs I should know?

A: Bermudians are known for being friendly and polite. Common courtesies:
- Greet people with "Good morning/afternoon/evening"
- Dress modestly when not at beaches or pools
- Ask permission before photographing people
- "Bermuda Time" can mean a relaxed pace - be patient

## Q: What about hurricanes?

A: Hurricane season is June 1 - November 30, with September being the most active month. The club has comprehensive storm procedures. Direct hits are rare due to Bermuda's location.

## Q: Is Bermuda tax-free for shopping?

A: Bermuda has no sales tax, making it attractive for shopping. However, import duties are included in retail prices. The duty-free shop at the airport offers savings on alcohol and tobacco.
